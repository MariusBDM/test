A Comprehensive Guide to Road Safety: Attitude

Master key driving strategies by maintaining a safe and courteous attitude on the road.

1. Courtesy on the Road

Being courteous on the road means being aware of your surroundings and treating other drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians with respect. Here are key behaviors to follow:

  • Yield when necessary: Even if you have the right of way, yield when it's safer to do so. Always give priority when it's legally required.
  • Use signals early: Signaling your intentions early helps other drivers understand your actions and avoid confusion.
  • Merge safely: Always allow room for vehicles to merge, especially in traffic. Blocking merging lanes can lead to accidents and congestion.
Read more about courteous driving on THINK! Road Safety

2. Avoiding Aggression

Driving aggressively can lead to dangerous situations. To avoid aggression:

  • Maintain a safe following distance: Avoid tailgating. Keeping a safe distance allows time to react and avoid collisions.
  • Stay calm and let aggressive drivers pass: If faced with aggressive driving, stay calm and avoid confrontation. Allow aggressive drivers to pass safely.
  • Avoid retaliation: Never respond to aggressive behavior with equally aggressive actions, such as speeding or cutting off other drivers.
Learn more about avoiding aggression on GOV.UK

3. Being Patient

Patience is key when driving. Avoiding unnecessary risks is vital for staying safe:

  • Never rush: Leave plenty of time to reach your destination to avoid feeling pressured to speed or take risks.
  • Yield to pedestrians: Always stop for pedestrians at crossings, even if they are slow or distracted.
  • Allow larger vehicles to maneuver: Buses and trucks need extra space. Be patient and give them room to turn or stop.
Read more about driving patiently in the Driver Alertness chapter

4. Interaction with Vulnerable Road Users

Drivers must be cautious around vulnerable road users, such as cyclists and pedestrians:

  • Slow down near schools and residential areas: Be extra vigilant for children and pedestrians crossing the road.
  • Give cyclists space: When overtaking a cyclist, ensure at least 1.5 meters of space.
  • Check for motorcyclists: Motorcycles can be difficult to spot in blind spots. Always check twice before changing lanes.

5. Dealing with Road Rage

Road rage can escalate quickly. Manage it by following these tips:

  • Stay calm: Avoid eye contact and do not engage with aggressive drivers.
  • Let aggressive drivers pass: Do not challenge aggressive drivers. Safely let them pass to avoid escalation.
  • Pull over if necessary: If threatened, pull over to a safe area and avoid further confrontation.

6. What You Should Not Do as a Driver (Attitude)

  • Do not retaliate against aggressive drivers. It can escalate the situation and cause accidents.

  • Avoid impatience. Rushing can lead to unsafe decisions, such as speeding or improper overtaking.
  • Do not ignore vulnerable road users. Pedestrians and cyclists require extra caution and space.
  • Do not refuse to yield when necessary, even if you have the legal right of way.
